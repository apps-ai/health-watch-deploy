18:Tae5,<p>The phrase "gut feeling" turns out to be more literal than we ever imagined. Your digestive system and brain are in constant communication via the gut-brain axis — a bidirectional highway of nerves, hormones, and immune signals.</p>
<h2>The Serotonin Surprise</h2>
<p>Here's a fact that surprises most people: approximately <strong>90% of your body's serotonin is produced in the gut</strong>, not the brain. While gut serotonin doesn't cross the blood-brain barrier, it profoundly influences gut motility and communicates mood signals to the brain via the vagus nerve.</p>
<p>Your gut microbiome — the 38 trillion bacteria that inhabit your intestines — plays a central role in regulating this system.</p>
<h2>What the Research Shows</h2>
<p>The landmark <strong>SMILES trial</strong> (2017) randomly assigned adults with major depression to either a Mediterranean-style dietary intervention or social support. After 12 weeks:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>32%</strong> of the diet group achieved remission vs <strong>8%</strong> of the control group</li>
<li>Mean depression scores dropped significantly more in the diet group</li>
<li>The dietary intervention cost less than the social support therapy</li>
</ul>
<p>This doesn't mean diet replaces medication or therapy. But it does mean it belongs in the conversation.</p>
<h2>The Microbiome-Mood Connection</h2>
<p>Key mechanisms through which gut bacteria influence mental health:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>GABA production</strong> — Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium species produce gamma-aminobutyric acid, your primary calming neurotransmitter</li>
<li><strong>Short-chain fatty acids</strong> — Fermentation of fibre produces butyrate, which reduces neuroinflammation</li>
<li><strong>Tryptophan metabolism</strong> — Gut bacteria influence how tryptophan (the precursor to serotonin) is metabolised</li>
</ol>
<h2>Foods That Support the Gut-Brain Axis</h2>
<h3>Fermented Foods (Daily)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Kefir, yogurt with live cultures</li>
<li>Kimchi, sauerkraut, miso</li>
<li>Kombucha (low sugar varieties)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Prebiotic Fibre (Aim for 25–35g/day)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Jerusalem artichoke, garlic, leeks, onions</li>
<li>Green bananas, oats, flaxseed</li>
</ul>
<h3>Omega-3 Fatty Acids</h3>
<ul>
<li>Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel) 2–3×/week</li>
<li>Walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseed oil</li>
</ul>
<h3>Polyphenols</h3>
<ul>
<li>Blueberries, dark chocolate (≥70%), green tea</li>
<li>Extra-virgin olive oil, colourful vegetables</li>
</ul>
<h2>A Practical Starting Point</h2>
<p>Rather than overhauling your diet overnight, try adding one fermented food and one prebiotic source per day for two weeks. Track your sleep quality and energy levels.
