Expert Q&A: Debunking Nutrition Myths with Dr. Okafor

We sat down with Dr. Sarah Okafor to discuss common misconceptions about protein, carbs, and meal timing.

Dr. Sarah Okafor
May 9, 20258 min read

Dr. Sarah Okafor

MD, MSc Sports Medicine

Dr. Sarah Okafor is a practicing sports medicine physician specializing in science-based nutrition.

Q: Is eating carbs late at night bad for fat loss?

A: No. Fat loss is determined by your overall daily caloric balance. Your body doesn't suddenly store carbs as fat just because the clock strikes 8 PM.

Q: How many eggs can I eat in a day?

A: For most healthy individuals, eating 1–2 eggs daily is perfectly fine and provides high-quality protein, choline, and essential vitamins without adversely affecting cholesterol levels.

Nutrition doesn't have to be complicated. In this interview, we address some of the most frequent questions we receive from our readers.
