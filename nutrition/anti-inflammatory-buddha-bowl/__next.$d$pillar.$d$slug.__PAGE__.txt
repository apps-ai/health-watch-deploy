14:Te2d,<h2>Why This Bowl Fights Inflammation</h2>
<p>Chronic low-grade inflammation is linked to a wide range of modern health conditions, including cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and autoimmune disorders. Diet is one of the most powerful levers we have to modulate the body's inflammatory response — and this bowl is designed to pull that lever hard in the right direction.</p>
<p>Each ingredient earns its place here based on evidence, not trend:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Turmeric</strong> contains curcumin, which inhibits the NF-κB molecular signalling pathway — one of the most important drivers of chronic inflammation in the body.</li>
<li><strong>Avocado</strong> is rich in oleic acid and monounsaturated fats that have been shown to lower levels of C-reactive protein (CRP), a key blood marker of systemic inflammation.</li>
<li><strong>Kale</strong> delivers vitamins K, C, and A, along with glucosinolates — plant compounds that support the body's antioxidant defence systems.</li>
<li><strong>Chickpeas</strong> provide resistant starch and soluble fibre, both of which feed beneficial gut bacteria and reduce intestinal permeability, a contributor to systemic inflammation.</li>
<li><strong>Extra virgin olive oil</strong> (in the tahini dressing and roasting) contains oleocanthal, a natural anti-inflammatory compound structurally similar to ibuprofen.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Nutritional Highlights</h2>
<p>This bowl provides over <strong>14g of plant protein</strong> per serving, a meaningful portion toward the 0.8–1.2g per kg of bodyweight that active adults require daily. The macronutrient balance — 420 kcal, 52g complex carbohydrates, 14g protein, 18g healthy fat — makes it suitable as a complete meal rather than a side dish.</p>
<p>Beyond macros, each bowl delivers:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>~8g of dietary fibre</strong> — supporting gut microbiome diversity and blood sugar regulation</li>
<li><strong>Significant vitamin K</strong> from kale — critical for bone health and anti-inflammatory signalling</li>
<li><strong>Folate</strong> from chickpeas and avocado — essential for cell repair and homocysteine metabolism</li>
<li><strong>Calcium and zinc</strong> from tahini — two minerals commonly underconsumed on plant-based diets</li>
<li><strong>Lutein and zeaxanthin</strong> from kale — protective antioxidants for eye and skin health</li>
</ul>
<h2>Tips for Building a Better Bowl</h2>
