The 30-Minute Anti-Inflammatory Buddha Bowl

Turmeric-roasted chickpeas, massaged kale, and tahini dressing — a weeknight meal that fights inflammation from the inside out.

Dr. Sarah Okafor
March 31, 20258 min read

🍳 Recipe Details

Prep Time: 10 mins | Cook Time: 20 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 400g Chickpeas (canned, drained)
  • 300g Brown rice (cooked)
  • 150g Kale leaves, de-stemmed
  • 1 tsp Turmeric
  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • 3 tbsp Tahini
  • 2 tbsp Lemon juice
  • 1, minced Garlic clove
  • 100g Cherry tomatoes
  • ½, diced Cucumber
  • 1, sliced Avocado

Nutrition (per serving)

  • Calories: 420 kcal
  • Protein: 14g
  • Carbohydrates: 52g
  • Fat: 18g

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Toss chickpeas with olive oil, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20 minutes until crispy.
  2. Massage kale with a pinch of salt and a drizzle of olive oil for 1 minute until softened.
  3. Whisk tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and 2–3 tbsp water until smooth dressing forms.
  4. Divide rice across 4 bowls. Top with kale, chickpeas, tomatoes, cucumber, and avocado.
  5. Drizzle tahini dressing generously. Serve immediately.

Why This Bowl Fights Inflammation

Chronic low-grade inflammation is linked to a wide range of modern health conditions, including cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and autoimmune disorders. Diet is one of the most powerful levers we have to modulate the body's inflammatory response — and this bowl is designed to pull that lever hard in the right direction.

Each ingredient earns its place here based on evidence, not trend:

  • Turmeric contains curcumin, which inhibits the NF-κB molecular signalling pathway — one of the most important drivers of chronic inflammation in the body.
  • Avocado is rich in oleic acid and monounsaturated fats that have been shown to lower levels of C-reactive protein (CRP), a key blood marker of systemic inflammation.
  • Kale delivers vitamins K, C, and A, along with glucosinolates — plant compounds that support the body's antioxidant defence systems.
  • Chickpeas provide resistant starch and soluble fibre, both of which feed beneficial gut bacteria and reduce intestinal permeability, a contributor to systemic inflammation.
  • Extra virgin olive oil (in the tahini dressing and roasting) contains oleocanthal, a natural anti-inflammatory compound structurally similar to ibuprofen.

Nutritional Highlights

This bowl provides over 14g of plant protein per serving, a meaningful portion toward the 0.8–1.2g per kg of bodyweight that active adults require daily. The macronutrient balance — 420 kcal, 52g complex carbohydrates, 14g protein, 18g healthy fat — makes it suitable as a complete meal rather than a side dish.

Beyond macros, each bowl delivers:

  • ~8g of dietary fibre — supporting gut microbiome diversity and blood sugar regulation
  • Significant vitamin K from kale — critical for bone health and anti-inflammatory signalling
  • Folate from chickpeas and avocado — essential for cell repair and homocysteine metabolism
  • Calcium and zinc from tahini — two minerals commonly underconsumed on plant-based diets
  • Lutein and zeaxanthin from kale — protective antioxidants for eye and skin health

Tips for Building a Better Bowl

This recipe is a template as much as it is a dish. Once you understand the formula, you can rotate ingredients seasonally and keep the anti-inflammatory impact high:

The base (complex carbohydrate): Brown rice works brilliantly, but so does quinoa, farro, buckwheat, or sweet potato cubes. Choose whole grains over refined wherever possible.

The greens (polyphenols + fibre): Massaged kale is the gold standard for texture and nutrition. Spinach, Swiss chard, or rocket all work well and require no cooking.

The protein (legumes or lean animal protein): Turmeric chickpeas are the star here, but white beans, lentils, or baked tempeh are equally nourishing substitutions.

The fat (anti-inflammatory oils): The tahini dressing and avocado supply generous monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats. A sprinkle of sesame seeds or pumpkin seeds adds crunch and additional healthy fats.
