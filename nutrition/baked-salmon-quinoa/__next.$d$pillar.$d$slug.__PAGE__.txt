17:T489,<h2>Heart-Healthy Fats and Complete Protein</h2>
<p>Salmon is one of the best sources of the long-chain <strong>omega-3 fatty acids</strong> EPA and DHA. These fats are essential because your body cannot produce them. They have been shown to reduce joint stiffness, improve cardiovascular profiles, and lower markers of muscle damage after intense exercise.</p>
<h2>Step-by-Step Cooking Guide</h2>
<p>This meal takes just 30 minutes to make, making it a perfect weeknight dinner option.</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Rinse and cook the quinoa</strong>: Rinse 100g of quinoa under cold water. Combine with 200ml of water in a small pot, bring to a boil, then cover and simmer for 15 minutes. Fluff with a fork.</li>
<li><strong>Season the salmon</strong>: Pat the salmon dry. Rub with olive oil, lemon juice, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.</li>
<li><strong>Bake</strong>: Place salmon and asparagus on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake at 200°C (400°F) for 12–15 minutes until the salmon is flakey.</li>
<li><strong>Assemble</strong>: Plate the quinoa, top with baked salmon, side with asparagus, and garnish with fresh dill and lemon zest.</li>
