Keto Diet Meal Plan: 7 Days of Sample Meals

Get a complete 7-day keto diet meal plan with macros, snack ideas, meal prep tips, and the most common meal-planning mistakes to avoid.

Alex Chen
Reviewed by sam-okonkwo, MD, MPH
July 12, 20268 min read

Meal planning is the difference between keto success and frustration. Without a clear plan, it's easy to reach for convenient foods that are secretly high in carbs, unknowingly break ketosis, and give up within the first two weeks. A well-designed 7-day keto meal plan removes the guesswork and sets you up for a smooth, effective start.

What Does a Keto Meal Plan Include?

A properly structured keto meal plan keeps daily net carbs under 20–30 grams, while distributing the remaining calories across fat (65–75%) and protein (20–30%). Every meal should be built around a quality protein source and healthy fat, with low-carb vegetables for fiber and micronutrients.

A good keto meal plan also accounts for:

  • Electrolytes: Sodium, potassium, and magnesium are depleted as insulin drops. Salt food generously and consider a magnesium supplement.
  • Meal timing: Most keto followers do well with 3 meals per day or 2 meals plus 1 snack. Intermittent fasting pairs well with keto.
  • Variety: Rotating protein sources and vegetables prevents nutrient gaps and flavor fatigue.

How Many Carbs, Fat, and Protein Per Day?

For a person with a daily calorie target of 1,800 calories on standard keto:

MacroPercentageGrams
Fat70%140g
Protein25%113g
Net Carbs5%22g

Use a keto macro calculator (entering your height, weight, activity level, and goal) to personalize these numbers. Protein is critical: too little leads to muscle loss, too much can impair ketosis through gluconeogenesis.

Sample 7-Day Keto Meal Plan

All meals target approximately 20–25g net carbs for the full day. Exact totals will vary by portion size.

Day 1

  • Breakfast: 3 scrambled eggs cooked in butter with 2 strips of bacon, ½ avocado
  • Lunch: Tuna salad (canned tuna, mayo, celery, pickles) served in romaine lettuce cups
  • Dinner: Pan-seared salmon fillet with garlic butter, steamed broccoli with olive oil
  • Snack: 1 oz sharp cheddar, 10 macadamia nuts

Day 2

  • Breakfast: Keto smoothie — heavy cream, 1 cup unsweetened almond milk, 1 scoop unflavored collagen, 1 tbsp MCT oil, handful of spinach
  • Lunch: Cobb salad — romaine, grilled chicken, hard-boiled egg, bacon, avocado, blue cheese, olive oil and vinegar
  • Dinner: Bunless grass-fed beef burgers with cheese, lettuce, tomato, and a side of sautéed zucchini
  • Snack: Celery sticks with almond butter

Day 3

  • Breakfast: Veggie omelet (3 eggs, spinach, mushrooms, bell pepper) with 1 tbsp butter
  • Lunch: Chicken Caesar salad (no croutons) with full-fat Caesar dressing
  • Dinner: Pork belly with roasted bok choy and sesame oil
  • Snack: Hard-boiled egg, ¼ cup almonds

Day 4

  • Breakfast: Full-fat Greek yogurt (plain, unsweetened) with 10 raspberries and crushed walnuts
  • Lunch: Egg salad stuffed in hollowed-out bell pepper halves
  • Dinner: Shrimp stir-fry with zucchini noodles, garlic, and olive oil
  • Snack: 1 oz cheese, 3–4 slices pepperoni

Day 5

  • Breakfast: Keto "bread" toast (almond flour base) with cream cheese and 2 fried eggs
  • Lunch: BLT salad — romaine, bacon, tomato, avocado, mayo dressing
  • Dinner: Baked chicken thighs with garlic herb butter, roasted asparagus
  • Snack: Pork rinds with guacamole (2 tbsp)

Day 6

  • Breakfast: Bacon and egg breakfast cups (baked in muffin tin with cheese)
  • Lunch: Greek salad with grilled chicken, feta, olives, cucumber, olive oil and lemon
  • Dinner: Lamb chops with roasted Brussels sprouts drizzled with bacon fat
  • Snack: Cucumber slices with cream cheese and smoked salmon

Day 7

  • Breakfast: Keto pancakes (almond flour, eggs, cream cheese, vanilla extract) with sugar-free whipped cream and a few blueberries
  • Lunch: Turkey and avocado lettuce wraps with cream cheese and cucumber
  • Dinner: Slow-cooker beef short ribs with cauliflower mash and steamed green beans
  • Snack: Mixed nuts (macadamia, pecan, walnuts), 1 square of 85%+ dark chocolate

Keto-Friendly Snack Ideas

When hunger strikes between meals, these snacks stay under 3–5g net carbs:

  • Hard-boiled eggs
  • Cheese cubes or string cheese
  • Macadamia nuts or pecans
  • Celery with almond or peanut butter
  • Cucumber with cream cheese
  • Pork rinds and guacamole
  • Olives
  • Deli turkey slices wrapped around cheese
  • 85%+ dark chocolate (1–2 squares)

How to Meal Prep for Keto

Successful keto meal prep follows a simple structure:

  1. Batch cook proteins on Sunday: Grill or bake chicken thighs, hard-boil eggs, cook ground beef, and portion salmon fillets.
  2. Pre-wash and chop vegetables: Store ready-to-use spinach, broccoli florets, sliced zucchini, and chopped bell peppers in containers.
  3. Make fat bombs or snacks in advance: Prepare cheese bites, fat bomb energy balls (coconut oil, almond butter, cocoa), or portion out nuts into snack bags.
  4. Prepare keto-friendly dressings: Caesar, ranch, and olive oil-based dressings stay fresh for 5–7 days in the fridge.
  5. Use a macro tracker for the first 4 weeks: Apps like Cronometer or Carb Manager help you stay within keto ratios until you build intuition.

Common Meal-Planning Mistakes

  1. Not tracking "hidden" carbs: Many condiments, sauces, and packaged foods contain 2–5g of carbs per serving. These add up quickly. Check labels obsessively at first.
  2. Eating too little protein: Aiming too low on protein (under 0.8g/lb body weight) leads to muscle breakdown, especially during weight loss.
  3. Neglecting vegetables entirely: Some beginners cut all carbs including low-carb vegetables. This leads to fiber deficiency, constipation, and micronutrient gaps.
  4. Not planning for social situations: Having a keto strategy for restaurants and social meals prevents derailments. Grilled proteins and salads work almost everywhere.
  5. Relying on keto-labeled processed foods: Many keto snack bars, cookies, and products have high calorie counts and low nutrient density. Real, whole keto foods produce better results.

A structured 7-day keto meal plan dramatically reduces the mental load of maintaining ketosis. Once you build familiarity with keto-friendly foods and your personal macro targets, meal planning becomes intuitive — and the benefits of keto become sustainable for the long term.
