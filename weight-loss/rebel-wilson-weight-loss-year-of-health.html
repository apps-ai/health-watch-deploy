Rebel Wilson's Weight Loss: Her 'Year of Health'

Rebel Wilson publicly declared a 'Year of Health' and shared details about her approach. Here's what she described doing, her motivations, and what experts say about long-term lifestyle change.

Sam Okonkwo
Reviewed by Alex Chen, MS, RD
July 13, 20267 min read

Introduction: Rebel Wilson's Publicly Documented Transformation

Rebel Wilson became one of the most openly documented celebrity weight loss stories of 2020 when she publicly declared her intention to have a "Year of Health" — announcing a specific goal weight of 165 lbs and sharing her progress regularly across social media throughout the year.

What set Wilson's story apart was the unprecedented transparency with which she approached it. Rather than a quiet transformation followed by a media reveal, she narrated the year in real time — creating a public accountability structure and allowing her audience to follow along with the ups and downs of a real health journey.

What Did Rebel Wilson Call Her 'Year of Health'?

Rebel Wilson coined the phrase herself on Instagram at the start of 2020, setting it as an explicit annual theme. Key elements of how she defined and pursued it:

  • A goal weight of 165 lbs: She stated this publicly — one of the few celebrities to name a specific target
  • Monthly updates: She tracked and shared her progress throughout the year on social media
  • Reaching her goal ahead of schedule: Wilson reported reaching her goal weight in November 2020 — two months before year end
  • The journey continued publicly: She has continued to share her ongoing wellness journey in subsequent years

The "Year of Health" framing is notable from a behavioral science perspective. Declaring a specific, time-bounded goal publicly creates accountability mechanisms that research shows significantly improve follow-through compared to private resolutions.

What Diet and Exercise Changes Did She Describe?

Wilson has been one of the more specific celebrities about what she actually did. Key elements she has referenced publicly include:

Exercise:

  • Hiking: Wilson frequently documented outdoor hikes on social media as a primary form of exercise — physically demanding, psychologically restorative, and requiring no gym membership
  • Personal training: She has referenced working with personal trainers, including during her time filming in various locations
  • High Intensity Interval Training (HIIT): She has mentioned HIIT workouts as part of her routine
  • Mayr Method clinic: Wilson spent time at the VivaMayr clinic in Austria, a medically supervised wellness retreat based on principles of gut health and mindful eating

Nutrition:

  • The Mayr Method (from the clinic) emphasizes slow eating, mindful consumption, gut health, and elimination of certain processed foods — though it is not a standard clinical protocol
  • Reduced alcohol consumption: She has referenced cutting back on alcohol as part of her approach
  • Focus on protein and reduced sugar: Consistent with the Mayr Method principles she described

What Motivated Her Approach?

Wilson's stated motivations evolved and were articulated clearly across her public narrative:

Fertility: A primary and specific motivation she named was her desire to improve her fertility health. She has spoken about wanting to have a biological child and being advised that losing weight would improve her chances — a medically supported connection.

Long-term health: Beyond fertility, she has spoken about wanting to feel better, have more energy, and be in better health for the long term.

Career and confidence: While she has not centered aesthetic goals, she has acknowledged that feeling physically better affected her confidence and energy for her career.

Self-challenge: The "Year of Health" framing itself suggests a desire for personal growth and achievement — treating health as something to actively pursue and accomplish.

These motivations represent the kind of intrinsic, meaningful goal-setting that behavioral science identifies as most predictive of sustained behavior change.

Healthy Takeaways: Expert Insights on Long-Term Lifestyle Change

Several aspects of Rebel Wilson's approach are well-supported by evidence and offer useful frameworks:

1. Public accountability is a legitimate behavior change strategy Research in behavioral economics and health psychology consistently shows that public commitment increases follow-through. By sharing her goals publicly, Wilson created social accountability — a formal structure with measurable consequences for non-adherence.

2. Outdoor activity has psychological advantages over indoor exercise Wilson's emphasis on hiking aligns with research showing that green exercise (physical activity in nature) produces superior mood outcomes compared to equivalent indoor exercise. People who enjoy their exercise are significantly more likely to maintain it long-term.

3. Mindful eating practices have genuine evidence The Mayr Method's emphasis on slow eating and mindful consumption aligns with research showing that eating slowly reduces calorie intake by improving recognition of satiety signals. The average person who eats slowly consumes 10–20% fewer calories at meals.

4. Alcohol reduction has meaningful metabolic effects Alcohol provides 7 calories per gram with no nutritional value and significantly impairs fat oxidation. Reducing alcohol consumption is associated with improved sleep quality, reduced calorie intake, and better metabolic function — all of which support weight management.

5. The fertility-weight connection is medically established Wilson's fertility motivation reflects a real clinical relationship. Excess body fat disrupts estrogen metabolism and can impair ovulation and implantation. For women with BMI above certain thresholds, weight loss can measurably improve fertility outcomes — making this a genuinely medically-motivated goal.

6. Medically supervised retreats: useful but not universally accessible The Mayr Method clinic represents a high-resource intervention. The evidence for the specific protocol is limited, but the core principles — gut health, mindful eating, reduced processed food — have broader support. The accessibility caveat is important: such approaches are not available to most people, and equivalent results are achievable through less expensive means.

Conclusion: Key Lessons From Her Journey

Rebel Wilson's "Year of Health" is one of the most documented and specific celebrity weight loss accounts available, which makes it more instructive than most. The most transferable lessons:

  • Name your goal specifically — a defined target creates clearer direction than vague intentions
  • Public accountability is a genuine tool — sharing goals (even with a friend or small group) significantly improves follow-through
  • Find activities you genuinely enjoy — Wilson's hiking approach represents sustainable movement, not just punishment exercise
  • Meaningful motivations outperform aesthetic ones — fertility, energy, longevity are more durable than "looking better"
  • The fundamental tools are standard — despite the Mayr Method and celebrity resources, Wilson's core approach used caloric deficit, exercise, and mindful eating — the same tools available to everyone
