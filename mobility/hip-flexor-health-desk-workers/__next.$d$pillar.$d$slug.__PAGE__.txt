10:Tafc,<p>The hip flexor complex — primarily the iliopsoas (psoas major + iliacus) — is designed to bring your thigh towards your torso. When you sit for hours, these muscles stay in a shortened position, gradually losing both their length and their ability to generate force at end range.</p>
<p>The result: anterior pelvic tilt, reduced stride length, increased lower back compression, and a shuffling gait that makes you look (and feel) older than you are.</p>
<h2>The Daily Desk Worker Damage</h2>
<p>Here's what happens at a cellular level during prolonged sitting:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Sarcomere number decreases</strong> — muscle fibres physically shorten by losing functional units</li>
<li><strong>Connective tissue stiffens</strong> — fascia becomes less pliable and more resistant to lengthening</li>
<li><strong>Neural activation patterns change</strong> — the hip flexors become neurologically dominant, while the glutes become inhibited</li>
</ul>
<p>This is often called "lower crossed syndrome" — a predictable pattern of tight hip flexors + lower back muscles paired with weak glutes + deep abdominals.</p>
<h2>The 4-Part Daily Routine (15 Minutes)</h2>
<h3>Part 1: The 90/90 Hip Flexor Release (3 min)</h3>
<p>Position yourself in a half-kneeling position with your front knee at 90° and back knee on the ground. Posteriorly tilt your pelvis (tuck your tailbone) and lean forward until you feel the stretch in your back hip.</p>
<ul>
<li>Hold 30 seconds, 3 sets per side</li>
<li><strong>Key cue</strong>: The stretch should be in the front of the back hip, not the lower back</li>
</ul>
<h3>Part 2: Supine Psoas Release — Dead Bug (4 min)</h3>
<p>Lie on your back with arms extended toward the ceiling and knees at 90°. Slowly lower one arm overhead while extending the opposite leg, maintaining lower back contact with the floor.</p>
<ul>
<li>8 reps per side, 2 sets</li>
<li>This trains the psoas under load through its full range</li>
</ul>
<h3>Part 3: Couch Stretch (4 min)</h3>
<p>Kneel facing away from a couch. Place your back shin on the couch seat and bring your front foot to a lunge position. Maintain upright torso.</p>
<ul>
<li>60 seconds per side, 2 sets</li>
<li>The deepest hip flexor stretch in this sequence</li>
</ul>
<h3>Part 4: Hip Flexor Strengthening — Tall Kneeling March (4 min)</h3>
<p>From tall kneeling, brace your core and lift one knee off the ground, bringing it to 90°. Hold 2 seconds, lower slowly.</p>
<ul>
<li>10 reps per side, 2 sets</li>
<li>This strengthens hip flexors through range, not just passively lengthens them</li>
</ul>
<h2>When to Do This Routine</h2>
<p>Ideally daily — morning or post-work are both effective.
